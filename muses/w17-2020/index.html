Reminder Reindeer

This week, I got lost in other people's priorities.

There were multiple emails trails to follow; there were some merges to be done with my teammate; some bugs popped up for me to fix on my software robot.

Add intermittent diarrhoea and subconscious coronavirus panic into the mix, and boom. I became very busy, but less productive than last week. I stopped using my calendar and, as a result, nearly forgot to attend some of my meetings. Nearly.

I also developed pain in the base of my ring finger as I typed, so I took more breaks than usual. That only increased my frustrations as I watched my list of to-dos grow.

Thankfully, I had put in place reminders to stop myself from falling off the track completely. One of those reminders is a practice highlighted in Brendon Burchard's book, High Performance Habits. Basically,

  1. Decide on at least three words you want to be/feel
  2. Set an alarm to go off at a certain time every day, with those three words as the alarm label
  3. When the alarm goes off, take time to remember and feel the meaning of those three words.

If you have more than three words, you can set multiple alarms throughout the day, each alarm labelled with different sets of words.

Grateful, Blessed, Unshakeable.

These are the words I'd set to pop up right before I break for lunch.

At first, I'd swipe off the notification and continue working - it felt like a distraction to me. Over time, however, I decided to view this notification as though it's a cat that's trying to get my attention.

When cats demand your attention, you have to give it to them. Thus, I started taking five seconds to breathe, feel, and relax before dismissing the notification. This deliberate act turned out to be a good distraction that gradually got me back on track. By Friday, I was able to continue my time-tracking practice and knock off a few items in my Trello board.

In the upcoming week, I'll get back to the habit of using my calendar.

Also, since I don't have a cat, and Reminder starts with R, I'm going to call my alarm app a Reminder Reindeer from now on :)

Toastmasters meeting on Saturday (today!) was fun, too! I was the Table Topics Master for this week's meeting and there was a bit of confusion for one particular instance (my fault). Otherwise, it went okay. I'm thankful that my clubmates are so encouraging. I'll do better next time!

This week, I pray that...

  • May the coronavirus pandemic die down around the world for good (no second wave pls)
  • May those who are being hit severely by the consequences of the pandemic receive enough aid to successfully tide over this period
  • May my carpal tunnel syndrome (I hope not, but I think that's what the finger pain is all about) recover completely in the upcoming month. I'll take care of you, just dont blow up :')
