What Color Is Your Identification Card?

When I received my identification card, or IC, back in 2015, I was told that it is a VIP - Very Important Plastic. In the words of my grandmother, if I lost my IC,

Someone is going to pick it up, use it to borrow money from loansharks, and thenâ€¦ tsk tsk tsk... your life will be hell!

I was terrified of the scenario my grandmother had described and, as a result, became hyper-aware of other people's ICs and my own.

For instance, when I queued at the clinic, I would observe that the person in front of me had a blue IC instead of a pink one like mine. Wikipedia told me that the blue IC is for Permanent Residents, while the pink one is for citizens.

Indeed, the IC is useful for identifying a person based on their "responses" to certain "questions" like nationality and gender. By this definition, all of us have a second IC. It's intangible - you can't see it, you can't touch it, but you can feel it - and we carry it with us all the time, even when we're sleeping.

"Tell me more about yourself."

If I use my physical IC to answer this question, my answer would be something like:

Hi, I'm Pei Qi, a female born on the 1st of January in the year 1999.

... which is something that will raise eyebrows, but not in a good way.

However, if I use my second IC to answer this question, my answer would sound much better:

Hi, I'm Pei Qi, a versatile software developer with strengths in web development and data science.

This is the question our second IC answers best.
