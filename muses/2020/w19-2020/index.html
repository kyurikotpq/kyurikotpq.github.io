安怎安呢款对待我?

Published on Sat May 09 2020

I thought last week was the last I'd see of the software robot (RPA) I had problems with.

I thought wrong.

At first, I thought that the Internet connection was unstable, causing the robot to die prematurely. However, after accounting for that scenario in a release on Wednesday, the error messages for disrupted Internet connections never came... and the robot still died prematurely.

Why?? Why do you do this to me?? 安怎安呢款对待我??

After 5 minutes, however, I snapped out of it, finally conceding that RPA deployment was less straightforward than web app deployment and I should stop saying "IT SHOULD WORK RIGHT?!". Successful RPA deployment requires taking care of many nuances... especially on the server side. But I had very little idea of what was happening on the server since my colleague, not me, was the one taking care of the deployment.

After some thought and with the help of my colleague, I was convinced that it wasn't the Internet connection, but rather the loading of the website I was spying on - the website loads much slower than the robot had anticipated. So I added Wait Stages to account for it.

Originally, I had placed a static Wait Stage (a mere timeout) for the object action. That's what I was taught in Blue Prism Foundation Training after all. However, my more-experienced colleagues informed me that I have to put conditions - in this case, an <element> check exists - inside the Wait Stage for it to be effective.

With my fingers crossed, I released this version of the RPA on Friday morning. Before I knocked off, it was still running (it's supposed to run 24/7 for a few days) and had managed to recover by itself upon several failures. Hopefully, I will be able to hand over the RPA on Monday.

I'm lactose intolerant?

I've been drinking milk since young and this has never happened to me before 2018, so I'm not sure what happened to my body. But I'll start monitoring my diet and episodes closely to see if there's a "tipping point" of sorts, i.e. how much milk I can drink without triggering severe episodes.
